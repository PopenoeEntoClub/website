100 years ago, Edwin A. Popenoe charted the Entomological Club as part of his new Department of Entomology at the Kansas State Agricultural College.  

Now, Kansas State University's Popenoe Entomology Club provides many student services, including: fellowship and professional development, travel funding opportunities, public education through demonstrations and the Insect Zoo, outreach and support of 4-H and FFA youth clubs.

  • We represent student interests in the department
  • Every semester, we sponsor a seminar from a noted entomologist  
  • We host the fall picnic and support department activies such as the KSU open house and Mudge week

Our mission is to promote activities that enhance interest and provide greater knownledge of entomology within our group and the community at large.

FFA Collections

The Popenoe Club sells insect collections as a service to FFA (Future Farmers of America) instructors. The collections includes specimens needed to study for the FFA Entomology Contest, which is sponsored by the Department of Entomology, Kansas State University

Fundraising Activities

Apart from selling insect collections to FFA, additional funds are raised by selling mugs and other merch at various events and online. The funds raised are used to support student travel and to help students meet resource needs.  

Tours of Department Facilities

The tours committee educates school groups and others interested in learning more about insects. The Insect Zoo, located in the old dairy barn by the K-State Gardens, is the focus of these tours. Graduate students conduct tours of the zoo, educating guests on both the living and preserved specimens on display.

Professional Development

Workshops are held monthly during club meeting times.  These events include informational panels from entomological experts and professionals, mock interviews, writing workshops, invited speakers, and more!
